title: Design Validation & FEA Study of a Mechanical Scissor Jack Lifting Arm (Self Directed Learning Project)
description: Executed a Static Stress Analysis on a critical load bearing Lower Lifting Arm of a Mechanical Scissor Jack assembly to validate its structural limits and optimize for real world application.
🔹Objective: Determine the Factor of Safety (FoS) of the lower lifting arm under a calculated automotive load of 8,000N.
🔹Tools: SolidWorks - SimulationXpress.
🔹Key Findings:
  •Structural Integrity: The design achieved a Factor of Safety of 55.4561 under standard loading.
  •Failure Analysis: Conducted destructive testing simulations, identifying that the component would withstand a theoretical maximum load of 443,652.5N (approx. 44 Tons, FoS = 1) before failing at the pin connections.
🔹Engineering Insights & Learning Outcomes:
  •Efficiency vs. Strength: While the design is structurally sound, the high FoS (55.4561) highlights that the component is "over engineered" compared to the industry standard FoS of 3.0 – 5.0 for lifting equipment.
  •Material Selection: Validated that while the modeled solid Alloy Steel provides immense strength, it is highly impractical for mass manufacturing due to weight and cost. This reinforced the importance of using stamped sheet metal for commercial automotive jacks to balance safety with manufacturing efficiency.
